About Me

I'm a junior at Northeastern University pursuing a dual major in Data Science and Economics with a minor in Environmental Science. I'm passionate about using data to tell stories — whether that means building predictive models, designing dashboards, or uncovering patterns in economic systems that shape the world we live in.

Outside the classroom, I compete as an NCAA Division I pole vaulter, a sport that has taught me discipline, resilience, and how to perform under pressure. Athletics has always been central to my life: I grew up playing gymnastics, soccer, and track, raised in a family where sport is a shared language. My older sister is a D1 softball player, and my younger brothers compete in lacrosse — so dinner-table conversation tends to drift toward game film and training blocks pretty quickly.

I'm also the President of Run Your City (RYC) Roxbury, a free, volunteer-run youth track program serving the Roxbury community in Boston. When I joined as Treasurer, the program had two to four regular participants. Over time, through outreach, social media, and partnership-building, we've grown to over fifteen signed-up youth athletes. Watching kids discover what their bodies can do — and build confidence doing it — is one of the most rewarding things I've ever been part of.

When I'm not studying or training, you'll find me hiking, surfing, baking sourdough, or listening to psychedelic rock. I grew up in San Diego, so the ocean never feels far away, even in Boston winters. I'm always curious about the intersection of data, environment, and human behavior — and I'm looking forward to putting those interests to work in sports analytics, environmental finance, and beyond.
