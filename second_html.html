What I Care About

A look at the passions, projects, and pursuits that drive me.

Data Science & Economics

My academic work sits at the intersection of data science and economics — two fields that, together, let me move between computational rigor and real-world meaning. I've built NLP frameworks to analyze 25 years of Amazon annual reports, designed econometric models using the FRED API to study how crude oil price shocks ripple through household financial behavior, and created an executive dashboard tracking Vietnam's economic indicators using World Bank data.

I'm especially drawn to problems where the stakes feel tangible: energy markets, environmental finance, community resource allocation. My technical toolkit includes Python (pandas, matplotlib, seaborn, plotly, Altair, scikit-learn, statsmodels), econometric modeling, and data visualization. I'm also comfortable with bootstrapping, propensity score matching, Monte Carlo simulation, and Value at Risk modeling.

One of my favorite projects was a spurious regression lab where I built a network graph showing false causal relationships between unrelated macroeconomic variables — a great reminder that correlation is not causation, and that data can lie beautifully if you let it.

Run Your City Roxbury

RYC Roxbury is a free, volunteer-run youth track program I lead as President. Our mission is simple: make athletics accessible to every kid in the Roxbury community, regardless of background or resources. Since I joined, we've grown from just two or three regulars to over fifteen registered youth athletes, with a full spring season underway.

My role involves coordinating coaches and volunteers, running partnership outreach with organizations like the Boston Athletic Association, the Reggie Lewis Center, and local schools, and managing social media and fundraising. It's taught me as much about leadership and community organizing as any class I've taken.
